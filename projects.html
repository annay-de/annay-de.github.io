Econometrics · 2026

Decision under Uncertainty: Squad Allocation & Match Simulation Engine

ECO-6810, Computational Thinking & AI for Economists, Ashoka University. Probabilistic ball-by-ball IPL simulation engine built on 278,205 deliveries (2008–2025) across 11 outcome classes. XGBoost model achieved log-loss of 1.7136 vs. empirical baseline of 1.8670 (~8.2% improvement). Deployed as a Streamlit app enabling analysts to simulate innings, compare XI combinations, and generate projected scorecards.

Forecasting · 2026

Forecasting India's GVA: Agriculture and Services Sectors

ECO-3401, Data Analytics & Machine Learning, Ashoka University. Dual forecasting pipeline using SARIMAX and an ML ensemble (XGBoost, LightGBM, Ridge; weighted 0.4:0.4:0.2) on 58 quarterly observations (FY2011–12 to FY2025–26). Implemented expanding-window cross-validation and Moving Block Bootstrap (1,000 trajectories). Ensemble CV MAPE: ~4.4% (agriculture), ~3.0% (services) — outperforming standalone SARIMAX on both sectors.

Econometrics · 2026

Contractualisation and Wage Differences in Indian Manufacturing

ECO-5401, Econometrics, Ashoka University. Used firm-level ASI 2023–24 data (41,700+ observations) to test whether contractualisation suppresses wages in organised manufacturing. IV/2SLS estimation instrumenting firm-level contractualisation with peer-average contractualisation at the 5-digit NIC level. Found that a 1 percentage point rise in contractualisation reduced daily wages by Rs. 2.34 (significant at 1%); OLS underestimated this effect by ~42.8%.

Azad Foundation & SABAR Institute · 2024

Access to Public Toilets for Women and Trans/Queer Persons in Kolkata

One of Kolkata's largest gendered sanitation studies. 7,578 cis women and 38 trans/queer persons surveyed by 300+ trained student investigators from eight colleges and two universities. Key findings: 72% of women paid to access public toilets; 53% reported absent locks; 38% of trans/queer respondents reported harassment; 51% felt inadequate sanitation affected their ability to work and earn. Received extensive media coverage; contributed to policy discussions on gender-sensitive sanitation and women's labour-force participation.
