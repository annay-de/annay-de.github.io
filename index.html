PhD Scholar · Junior Research Fellow, Ashoka University Research Assistant, Centre for Social and Behaviour Change, Ashoka University UGC NET Qualified (Assistant Professor)

I am a PhD scholar in Economics at Ashoka University.

I work at the intersection of labour economics, political economy, and development, using field experiments, IV estimation, and large-scale surveys to study how institutions and social structures shape economic behaviour.

My current work examines intra-household bargaining, son-preference priming, and women's decision-making in rural Bihar.

Alongside my dissertation, I collaborate with SABAR Institute and Know Your Neighbour on applied field research on migration, minority welfare, urban inequality, and gendered public infrastructure.

About

I use field experiments, econometric methods, and large-scale primary surveys to study questions most textbooks do not quite reach.

I care about why people vote the way they do, who gets left out of growth stories, and how economic institutions interact with caste, gender, and geography.

Beyond research, I have built training programmes and workshops in data collection, AI tools, and data visualisation for students and researchers across West Bengal.

I am also the Founder of the Quizzing Society at the Department of Economics, University of Calcutta.
